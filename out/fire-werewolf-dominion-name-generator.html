The phonetics of werewolf Dominion names should feel weighty and ancient. Use hard consonants and deep vowels to convey the permanence of the fire force. Avoid soft, melodic sounds unless they specifically align with the chosen fire aesthetic.

Naming Rules for Fire Werewolf Dominion

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Fire theme. Every werewolf dominion name incorporates fire elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the dominion's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Werewolf naming traditions. Werewolf names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish werewolf names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Dominion scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple werewolf dominions for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A dominion called "The Fire Werewolf Dominion" might be known colloquially as "The Firehold" by its inhabitants.
