Suffix selection defines the type of Faction governance. Different endings convey different political structures — whether a sprawling empire, a close-knit clan, or a sacred realm.

Prefix choices are critical in undead Faction naming. A arcane-themed prefix sets the emotional tone and signals the faction's alignment, whether it leans toward arcane might, arcane wisdom, or arcane mystery.

The phonetics of undead Faction names should feel weighty and ancient. Use hard consonants and deep vowels to convey the permanence of the arcane force. Avoid soft, melodic sounds unless they specifically align with the chosen arcane aesthetic.

Naming Rules for Arcane Undead Faction

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Arcane theme. Every undead faction name incorporates arcane elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the faction's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Undead naming traditions. Undead names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish undead names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Faction scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple undead factions for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A faction called "The Arcane Undead Faction" might be known colloquially as "The Arcanehold" by its inhabitants.
