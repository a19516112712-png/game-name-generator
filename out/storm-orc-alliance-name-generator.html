The phonetics of orc Alliance names should feel weighty and ancient. Use hard consonants and deep vowels to convey the permanence of the storm force. Avoid soft, melodic sounds unless they specifically align with the chosen storm aesthetic.

Naming Rules for Storm Orc Alliance

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Storm theme. Every orc alliance name incorporates storm elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the alliance's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Orc naming traditions. Orc names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish orc names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Alliance scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple orc alliances for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A alliance called "The Storm Orc Alliance" might be known colloquially as "The Stormhold" by its inhabitants.
