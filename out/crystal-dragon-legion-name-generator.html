Mixing dragon and crystal elements should feel natural, not forced. The most evocative names arise when the crystal theme authentically complements the dragon cultural identity.

Many dragon Legion names follow a compound structure: a crystal-themed prefix paired with a Legion-specific suffix. This pattern creates names that are instantly recognizable as belonging to the dragon crystal tradition.

Naming Rules for Crystal Dragon Legion

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Crystal theme. Every dragon legion name incorporates crystal elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the legion's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Dragon naming traditions. Dragon names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish dragon names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Legion scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple dragon legions for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A legion called "The Crystal Dragon Legion" might be known colloquially as "The Crystalhold" by its inhabitants.
