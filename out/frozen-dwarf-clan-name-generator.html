Naming Rules for Frozen Dwarf Clan

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Frozen theme. Every dwarf clan name incorporates frozen elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the clan's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Dwarf naming traditions. Dwarf names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish dwarf names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Clan scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple dwarf clans for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A clan called "The Frozen Dwarf Clan" might be known colloquially as "The Frozenhold" by its inhabitants.
