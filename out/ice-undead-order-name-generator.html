Naming Guide

The phonetics of undead Order names should feel weighty and ancient. Use hard consonants and deep vowels to convey the permanence of the ice force. Avoid soft, melodic sounds unless they specifically align with the chosen ice aesthetic.

Many undead Order names follow a compound structure: a ice-themed prefix paired with a Order-specific suffix. This pattern creates names that are instantly recognizable as belonging to the undead ice tradition.

Prefix choices are critical in undead Order naming. A ice-themed prefix sets the emotional tone and signals the order's alignment, whether it leans toward ice might, ice wisdom, or ice mystery.

A undead Order name should tell a story at a glance. Consider what the ice theme says about the order's geography, history, and values. The name becomes a shorthand for the entire order's identity.

Mixing undead and ice elements should feel natural, not forced. The most evocative names arise when the ice theme authentically complements the undead cultural identity.

Suffix selection defines the type of Order governance. Different endings convey different political structures — whether a sprawling empire, a close-knit clan, or a sacred realm.

When creating names for a Ice Undead Order, the most important principle is to capture the essence of ice culture while honoring undead traditions. Start with a strong root word that reflects the ice energy of the order, then add a suffix that grounds the name in undead heritage.

The best undead Order names balance uniqueness with familiarity. While every ice order should have a distinct identity, the name should still feel like it belongs within the broader undead naming ecosystem.

Naming Rules for Ice Undead Order

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Ice theme. Every undead order name incorporates ice elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the order's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Undead naming traditions. Undead names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish undead names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Order scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple undead orders for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A order called "The Ice Undead Order" might be known colloquially as "The Icehold" by its inhabitants.
