The phonetics of orc Order names should feel weighty and ancient. Use hard consonants and deep vowels to convey the permanence of the void force. Avoid soft, melodic sounds unless they specifically align with the chosen void aesthetic.

Naming Rules for Void Orc Order

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Void theme. Every orc order name incorporates void elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the order's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Orc naming traditions. Orc names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish orc names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Order scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple orc orders for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A order called "The Void Orc Order" might be known colloquially as "The Voidhold" by its inhabitants.
