Naming Guide

The best dwarf Legion names balance uniqueness with familiarity. While every crystal legion should have a distinct identity, the name should still feel like it belongs within the broader dwarf naming ecosystem.

Prefix choices are critical in dwarf Legion naming. A crystal-themed prefix sets the emotional tone and signals the legion's alignment, whether it leans toward crystal might, crystal wisdom, or crystal mystery.

Many dwarf Legion names follow a compound structure: a crystal-themed prefix paired with a Legion-specific suffix. This pattern creates names that are instantly recognizable as belonging to the dwarf crystal tradition.

When creating names for a Crystal Dwarf Legion, the most important principle is to capture the essence of crystal culture while honoring dwarf traditions. Start with a strong root word that reflects the crystal energy of the legion, then add a suffix that grounds the name in dwarf heritage.

Mixing dwarf and crystal elements should feel natural, not forced. The most evocative names arise when the crystal theme authentically complements the dwarf cultural identity.

The phonetics of dwarf Legion names should feel weighty and ancient. Use hard consonants and deep vowels to convey the permanence of the crystal force. Avoid soft, melodic sounds unless they specifically align with the chosen crystal aesthetic.

A dwarf Legion name should tell a story at a glance. Consider what the crystal theme says about the legion's geography, history, and values. The name becomes a shorthand for the entire legion's identity.

Suffix selection defines the type of Legion governance. Different endings convey different political structures — whether a sprawling empire, a close-knit clan, or a sacred realm.

Naming Rules for Crystal Dwarf Legion

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Crystal theme. Every dwarf legion name incorporates crystal elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the legion's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Dwarf naming traditions. Dwarf names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish dwarf names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Legion scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple dwarf legions for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A legion called "The Crystal Dwarf Legion" might be known colloquially as "The Crystalhold" by its inhabitants.
