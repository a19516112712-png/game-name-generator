Naming Guide

A undead Legion name should tell a story at a glance. Consider what the ancient theme says about the legion's geography, history, and values. The name becomes a shorthand for the entire legion's identity.

When creating names for a Ancient Undead Legion, the most important principle is to capture the essence of ancient culture while honoring undead traditions. Start with a strong root word that reflects the ancient energy of the legion, then add a suffix that grounds the name in undead heritage.

Many undead Legion names follow a compound structure: a ancient-themed prefix paired with a Legion-specific suffix. This pattern creates names that are instantly recognizable as belonging to the undead ancient tradition.

The best undead Legion names balance uniqueness with familiarity. While every ancient legion should have a distinct identity, the name should still feel like it belongs within the broader undead naming ecosystem.

Prefix choices are critical in undead Legion naming. A ancient-themed prefix sets the emotional tone and signals the legion's alignment, whether it leans toward ancient might, ancient wisdom, or ancient mystery.

Mixing undead and ancient elements should feel natural, not forced. The most evocative names arise when the ancient theme authentically complements the undead cultural identity.

Suffix selection defines the type of Legion governance. Different endings convey different political structures — whether a sprawling empire, a close-knit clan, or a sacred realm.

The phonetics of undead Legion names should feel weighty and ancient. Use hard consonants and deep vowels to convey the permanence of the ancient force. Avoid soft, melodic sounds unless they specifically align with the chosen ancient aesthetic.

Naming Rules for Ancient Undead Legion

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Ancient theme. Every undead legion name incorporates ancient elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the legion's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Undead naming traditions. Undead names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish undead names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Legion scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple undead legions for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A legion called "The Ancient Undead Legion" might be known colloquially as "The Ancienthold" by its inhabitants.
