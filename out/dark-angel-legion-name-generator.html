Naming Guide

The phonetics of angel Legion names should feel weighty and ancient. Use hard consonants and deep vowels to convey the permanence of the dark force. Avoid soft, melodic sounds unless they specifically align with the chosen dark aesthetic.

Prefix choices are critical in angel Legion naming. A dark-themed prefix sets the emotional tone and signals the legion's alignment, whether it leans toward dark might, dark wisdom, or dark mystery.

Many angel Legion names follow a compound structure: a dark-themed prefix paired with a Legion-specific suffix. This pattern creates names that are instantly recognizable as belonging to the angel dark tradition.

Suffix selection defines the type of Legion governance. Different endings convey different political structures — whether a sprawling empire, a close-knit clan, or a sacred realm.

A angel Legion name should tell a story at a glance. Consider what the dark theme says about the legion's geography, history, and values. The name becomes a shorthand for the entire legion's identity.

Mixing angel and dark elements should feel natural, not forced. The most evocative names arise when the dark theme authentically complements the angel cultural identity.

When creating names for a Dark Angel Legion, the most important principle is to capture the essence of dark culture while honoring angel traditions. Start with a strong root word that reflects the dark energy of the legion, then add a suffix that grounds the name in angel heritage.

The best angel Legion names balance uniqueness with familiarity. While every dark legion should have a distinct identity, the name should still feel like it belongs within the broader angel naming ecosystem.

Naming Rules for Dark Angel Legion

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Dark theme. Every angel legion name incorporates dark elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the legion's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Angel naming traditions. Angel names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish angel names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Legion scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple angel legions for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A legion called "The Dark Angel Legion" might be known colloquially as "The Darkhold" by its inhabitants.
