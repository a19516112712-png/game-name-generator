Naming Rules for Blood Giant Alliance

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Blood theme. Every giant alliance name incorporates blood elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the alliance's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Giant naming traditions. Giant names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish giant names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Alliance scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple giant alliances for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A alliance called "The Blood Giant Alliance" might be known colloquially as "The Bloodhold" by its inhabitants.
