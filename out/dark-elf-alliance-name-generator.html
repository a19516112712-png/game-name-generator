A elf Alliance name should tell a story at a glance. Consider what the dark theme says about the alliance's geography, history, and values. The name becomes a shorthand for the entire alliance's identity.

Naming Rules for Dark Elf Alliance

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Dark theme. Every elf alliance name incorporates dark elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the alliance's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Elf naming traditions. Elf names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish elf names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Alliance scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple elf alliances for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A alliance called "The Dark Elf Alliance" might be known colloquially as "The Darkhold" by its inhabitants.
