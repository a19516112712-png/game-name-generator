Naming Guide

A undead Faction name should tell a story at a glance. Consider what the savage theme says about the faction's geography, history, and values. The name becomes a shorthand for the entire faction's identity.

Mixing undead and savage elements should feel natural, not forced. The most evocative names arise when the savage theme authentically complements the undead cultural identity.

Prefix choices are critical in undead Faction naming. A savage-themed prefix sets the emotional tone and signals the faction's alignment, whether it leans toward savage might, savage wisdom, or savage mystery.

The phonetics of undead Faction names should feel weighty and ancient. Use hard consonants and deep vowels to convey the permanence of the savage force. Avoid soft, melodic sounds unless they specifically align with the chosen savage aesthetic.

Many undead Faction names follow a compound structure: a savage-themed prefix paired with a Faction-specific suffix. This pattern creates names that are instantly recognizable as belonging to the undead savage tradition.

Suffix selection defines the type of Faction governance. Different endings convey different political structures — whether a sprawling empire, a close-knit clan, or a sacred realm.

When creating names for a Savage Undead Faction, the most important principle is to capture the essence of savage culture while honoring undead traditions. Start with a strong root word that reflects the savage energy of the faction, then add a suffix that grounds the name in undead heritage.

The best undead Faction names balance uniqueness with familiarity. While every savage faction should have a distinct identity, the name should still feel like it belongs within the broader undead naming ecosystem.

Naming Rules for Savage Undead Faction

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Savage theme. Every undead faction name incorporates savage elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the faction's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Undead naming traditions. Undead names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish undead names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Faction scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple undead factions for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A faction called "The Savage Undead Faction" might be known colloquially as "The Savagehold" by its inhabitants.
