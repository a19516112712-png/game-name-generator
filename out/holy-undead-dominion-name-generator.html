The phonetics of undead Dominion names should feel weighty and ancient. Use hard consonants and deep vowels to convey the permanence of the holy force. Avoid soft, melodic sounds unless they specifically align with the chosen holy aesthetic.

Prefix choices are critical in undead Dominion naming. A holy-themed prefix sets the emotional tone and signals the dominion's alignment, whether it leans toward holy might, holy wisdom, or holy mystery.

Naming Rules for Holy Undead Dominion

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Holy theme. Every undead dominion name incorporates holy elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the dominion's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Undead naming traditions. Undead names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish undead names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Dominion scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple undead dominions for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A dominion called "The Holy Undead Dominion" might be known colloquially as "The Holyhold" by its inhabitants.
