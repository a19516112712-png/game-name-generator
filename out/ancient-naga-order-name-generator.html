The phonetics of naga Order names should feel weighty and ancient. Use hard consonants and deep vowels to convey the permanence of the ancient force. Avoid soft, melodic sounds unless they specifically align with the chosen ancient aesthetic.

Naming Rules for Ancient Naga Order

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Ancient theme. Every naga order name incorporates ancient elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the order's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Naga naming traditions. Naga names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish naga names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Order scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple naga orders for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A order called "The Ancient Naga Order" might be known colloquially as "The Ancienthold" by its inhabitants.
