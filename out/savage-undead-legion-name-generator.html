Mixing undead and savage elements should feel natural, not forced. The most evocative names arise when the savage theme authentically complements the undead cultural identity.

The best undead Legion names balance uniqueness with familiarity. While every savage legion should have a distinct identity, the name should still feel like it belongs within the broader undead naming ecosystem.

Naming Rules for Savage Undead Legion

Rule 1: The name must reflect the Savage theme. Every undead legion name incorporates savage elements in its prefix or root word, establishing the legion's magical or elemental alignment.

Rule 2: Honor Undead naming traditions. Undead names follow specific phonetic patterns — certain consonant clusters, vowel preferences, and syllable structures that distinguish undead names from those of other races.

Rule 3: Match the Legion scale. Empire names should sound grand and sweeping; guild names should feel tight-knit and purposeful; clan names should evoke kinship and shared heritage.

Rule 4: Maintain internal consistency. If you generate multiple undead legions for the same world, ensure their names share linguistic DNA while remaining distinct enough to avoid confusion.

Rule 5: Consider the name's evolution. Great names often have shortened forms, nicknames, or historical variants. A legion called "The Savage Undead Legion" might be known colloquially as "The Savagehold" by its inhabitants.
